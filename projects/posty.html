reimagining composting to be human-centered, data-driven, and delightful in the year 2030

Over a span of 48 hours, 2 designers and I participated in Designprenuer's AI Design Hackathon, where we were prompted to envision ourselves in the world of 2030 and to use AI to mediate between human and ecological needs in urban spaces.

so... what's the problem?

Composting in NYC is broken. You’re legally required to do it, but most people either don’t know how, don’t care, or find it too much work. So we asked: what if composting wasn’t just required… what if it was fun?

JTBD

We needed a solution that meets people where they are – and make them actually want to compost

Meet Posty - a lovable AI-powered compost bot that helps turn your banana peels into park soil, your leftovers into school heat, and your scraps into stories.

Posty is our way of protecting the city we love — while making it easier for people to join in. It’s the only solution that blends AI, community data, and joyful behavior change — and it works because it feels human.

understanding the problem space

After my team and I decided to remediate composting with the help of Posty, we did a lot of ideating and research.

NYC produces over 1 million tons of food waste each year. And 97% of that goes to landfill or incineration — creating methane, a greenhouse gas over 80x more harmful than CO₂ in the short term.

We tried to fully understand how composting works, who and what it affects, and how our solution can create real impact.

We realized that with our solution, we can create a circular ecosystem, but with the help of our mentors, we also understood that we needed to heavily narrow down our user narrative.

understanding user needs

After talking with my team, we faced a bit of a block – we had so many ideas, but didn't know how to narrow down who would be impacted from our solution. So we went back to square one and decided to talk with real people.

We visited Union Square's drop-off and spoke to the Lower East Side Ecology Center. They want to grow — but they’re underfunded and overwhelmed.

We also talked with New Yorkers around the area to hear their thoughts on composting.

They told us it was too gross, took too much work, and that it didn't matter.

We also benchmarked AI food waste competitors such as Lomi, Winnow, and EvoBin.

But none of them offered a joyful, human-centered interaction that changes behavior. Posty isn’t just a bin — it’s a community-powered compost movement, built to scale.

building our narrative

From there, our narrative was born: We decided to tell our story with Frank, a 26 year old male who has never composted before, doesn't really know how it works, and doesn't care about how his impact could change the world.

After being warned of a fine for not composting, Frank says this: "If composting is now mandatory in NYC and I’ll be fined if I don’t do it, why can’t there be an easy way for me to do so while also benefiting at the same time?"

from here, we asked ourselves...

HMW

make a composting system that is so easy, so rewarding, and so fun that people actually want to do it?

To explore potential solutions, we started building our flow and sketching out our mobile screens.

With every step of our system's map, we kept Frank in mind and designed to spark incentive, joy, and emotions.

And while my teammates were starting to build our mobile screens, I began sketching what Posty would look like.

Using Sora AI, one of my teammates and I started generating images to build our storyboarded video demo.

final solutions: how posty works

Posty makes composting feel like a game!

1. Compost

Fill your Bucky bucket and drop off your compost to our nearest locations

2. Track

Posty's AI turns your compost into visible impact, converting your food scraps to points

3. Earn

Lastly, collect your points and claim your rewards.

my role

During this project, I led the visual and motion direction — designing Posty, Bucky, and Dumpy (yes, they’re a trio!). Beyond just visuals, I worked closely with the team to ensure every interaction — from our app screens to our microinteractions — felt systemic but emotionally resonant.

the result

My team and I were honored to receive first place at the AI Design Hackathon for our solution.

and on top op that I learned that...

even technical, structured systems (like composting) can create emotional connections

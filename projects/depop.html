designing a more personalized shopping experience for depop

Depop is an online second-hand shopping app where users can buy or sell clothes. Over five weeks, I set out to enhance Depop's buyer experience by introducing new customization features.

3

discovering the problem

As a frequent Depop user, I noticed a lack of personalized shopping experiences. To validate this, I conducted preliminary research on Depop's user interface, online reviews, and user flows.

understanding user needs

To uncover user pain points, I conducted three user interviews with Depop's target audience (18-26 year olds), aiming to understand their experiences with trustworthiness and customization. I used an interview script with 30 open-ended questions centered around engagement, personalization, trust, and usability.

these are the major takeaways:

insight 1
users go into the app and don’t end up buying anything
insight 2
users feel like their experience is not customized in an organized fashion
insight 3
there is a lack of trust between the seller and the buyer

sketching

To explore potential solutions, I facilitated a brainstorming session with four other designers. We focused on the question: “How might we give users a more customizable experience?” This led to ideas for profile customization, an outfit generator, and enhanced search filters.

prototyping & user testing

Using Figma, I created mid-fidelity prototypes of the new features and conducted usability tests with three users who matched our target audience.

systems map

These insights inspired me to rebuild Depop's current systems map, including both the seller and buyer flows. After I synthesized my findings from the usability testing, I edited my system's map to visualize where my new features would fit inside their current existing flow.

edge case

In case the user skips the onboarding quiz, they can edit their preferences manually on the home page to customize the outfits the generator creates. These filters will automatically apply to the user's search feed as well, reducing any repetitive actions.

learnings

If I could do this project again, I would create more initial low-fi sketches exploring different features, play with some loading bar animations like Depop does on the current app, and lastly, interview sellers on Depop to understand the differences in the user journeys.

next steps

For my next steps, I would like to explore my other insight which was user-seller trust, add more edge cases to my other features, and explore the generator concept further to have accurate and inclusive representation of all body types.
